Algorithm 1 - EXACT Algorithm 4 - Core-Exact

The Densest Subgraph Discovery (DSD) problem is foundational in graph mining, with applications in network science, biological analysis, and graph databases. DSD aims to find a subgraph with the highest density, defined either by edge-density or h-clique-density.

Traditional approaches face scalability challenges, especially with large datasets. This paper proposes efficient core-based exact and approximation algorithms leveraging k-core and (k, Ψ)-core concepts to solve DSD efficiently.
